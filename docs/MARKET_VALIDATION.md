# Hearthshade - Market Validation & Critic-Cycle Log

This document records the live-signal research, traction proof points, and the internal review-and-critic
cycles that promoted **Dark-Cozy (cozy-horror)** to the studio's **#1 underserved niche for 2026-2027**.

## 1. The thesis in one line

> A content-rich, polished **dark-cozy life-sim** sits in a quadrant with **proven, growing demand** (cozy +
> horror, both top-performing) but **near-zero flagship supply** (only micro-budget mood pieces exist today).

## 4. Supply gap (the moat)

The cozy-horror shelf today is **micro-budget mood pieces and pixel EA projects**. There is **no
content-complete, narrative-rich, mechanically-deep flagship** that treats dread as a managed resource with a
full life-sim wrapped around it. The blend is the moat: the capsule leads with the *horror* hook so we don't
drown in the saturated "cozy" tag, while still inheriting cozy's broad, growing, female-positive audience.

## 5. Internal critic-cycle log (the 10 -> 2 funnel)

1. **Dark-cozy life-sim (cozy-horror)** - *KEEP -> #1.* Demand proven, supply thin, blend defensible, no twitch skill (broad TAM).
2. Constructive co-op "friendslop" (non-horror) - *KEEP -> #2 (repo `sloop-troop`).*
3. Factory/automation sim - *CUT.* 2026 is "absolutely stacked" with factory builders.
4. Deckbuilder roguelike (Balatro-like) - *CUT.* "Lightning in a bottle," hard to replicate; saturated.
5. Open-world survival craft - *CUT.* Just fell out of the Steam top-10 genre list; high budget, crowded.
6. Pure cozy farming sim - *CUT.* Lives in Stardew's shadow + a sea of clones.
7. Story-rich narrative - *CAUTION.* Top genre but saturated; discoverability brutal without a hook.
8. Solo analog/found-footage horror - *CAUTION.* Rising but flooded with micro-budget entries; low moat.
9. Cozy-automation hybrid (Timberborn-like) - *CAUTION.* Overlaps the crowded automation space.
10. Social-deduction/party (Among Us-like) - *CUT.* Winner-take-all; viral lottery, not a repeatable bet.

**Critic challenges raised against #1 and how the design answers them:**
- *"Tone whiplash will feel incoherent."* -> Shared-toolbox rule + telegraphed dusk + difficulty bands.
- *"Permadeath repels the cozy core."* -> Opt-in only; default *Tended* makes loss preventable; *Gentle* removes it.
- *"'Cozy' is saturated."* -> We compete on the *dread* differentiator; the intersection is under-supplied.
- *"No combat = no retention hook."* -> Retention from life-sim depth + seasonal escalation + relationship stakes, plus a night-tension layer for clips/streams.

**Profitability read:** comps (Grimshire, Spiritfarer, Coral Island) clear the multi-hundred-k to multi-million
unit band at $15-25 premium; the blend lowers competition while inheriting cozy's broad TAM. Meets the
studio's **>60% profitability-confidence + multi-million revenue-potential** bar.
